DOL regulations on fee disclosure can be daunting, but they don't have to be.
 
Receiving your fee disclosures is just the start of your requirement under the new 408(b)2 ERISA regulations ... and the DOL is eager to see if you comply!
 
A recent article in PLANSPONSOR* outlined that, among other things, a DOL investigator will look for the following to assure you have a process to determine if your fees are reasonable:
  • All copies of required fee disclosures
  • All written contracts and agreements with service providers
  • All minutes of all board, plan trust, and committee meetings discussing the plan.
That's just to get the ball rolling, as the next and most important line of inquiry - and the focus of many lawsuits surrounding retirement plans - is whether the fees are reasonable.
 
How many plan sponsors are ready for that kind of inquiry on a moment's notice? Where does your company stand?
